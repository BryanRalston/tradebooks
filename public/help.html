Core Workflows

The five things you'll do most often — click any card to see step-by-step instructions.

💸

Track an Expense

Log what you spent — materials, subs, fuel, anything job-related.

  1. 1
    Open Expenses Tap Expenses in the sidebar (desktop) or the bottom tab bar (phone).
  2. 2
    Click "Add Expense" The big blue button at the top right. A form slides in — this is where you enter the details.
  3. 3
    Fill in the details Enter the date, amount, vendor (who you paid), and pick a category. Link it to a job if it belongs to one — this is important for profitability tracking later.
  4. 4
    Upload your receipt photo Tap the camera/upload area and snap a photo or pick one from your camera roll. Your receipt is now stored with the record — no more lost paper.
    💡 Snap the photo right after you pay. Takes 5 seconds and saves headaches at tax time.
  5. 5
    Save it Hit Save Expense. The expense shows up instantly in your list and rolls up to your dashboard totals.
💰

Record a Payment

Track money coming in — deposits, progress payments, final pay, anything.

  1. 1
    Open Income Tap Income in the navigation menu.
  2. 2
    Click "Add Income" Opens the income entry form.
  3. 3
    Enter the payment details Date, amount, payer (who paid you), and a description — like "Kitchen remodel deposit" or "Final draw, 123 Oak Street."
  4. 4
    Link it to a job Select the job from the dropdown. This connects the payment to the right project so your per-job profit numbers are accurate.
    💡 Always link payments to a job. It makes profitability reports actually useful.
  5. 5
    Save it Your income is recorded. The dashboard updates automatically.
💼

Create a Job

Set up a project so you can track all its costs and payments in one place.

  1. 1
    Open Jobs Tap Jobs in the nav.
  2. 2
    Click "New Job" The job creation form opens.
  3. 3
    Name the job and pick your client Use something descriptive — the address or project type works well. Link it to the right client from the dropdown.
  4. 4
    Set a budget (optional) Enter your contract amount or budget. TradeBooks will track your actual spending against it so you can see at a glance whether you're on track.
  5. 5
    Save and start tracking The job is live. Now tag expenses and income to this job as the project moves forward.
🧾

Send an Invoice

Bill your client professionally — download a PDF and send it by text or email.

  1. 1
    Open Invoices Tap Invoices in the nav.
  2. 2
    Click "New Invoice" Pick your client and the job this invoice is for.
  3. 3
    Add line items Each line is a thing you're billing for — labor, materials, equipment, whatever makes sense. Give it a description, quantity, and rate. The total calculates automatically.
  4. 4
    Set tax and due date If you charge sales tax, enter the rate and it'll be calculated on the total. Set your payment terms (e.g., due in 30 days).
  5. 5
    Save and download the PDF Hit Save Invoice, then click the PDF button. You'll get a professional-looking invoice with your business name and all the details.
    💡 Text the PDF directly to your client. Most homeowners respond faster to a text than an email.
  6. 6
    Mark it sent, then paid when you collect Update the invoice status as it moves — Draft → Sent → Paid. When you mark it Paid, that payment automatically records in your Income.
📊

Run a Tax Report

Get your numbers ready for your accountant — or file it yourself.

  1. 1
    Open Reports Tap Reports in the nav.
  2. 2
    Select Tax Summary This is your Schedule C-aligned summary — income and expenses broken down by category, exactly how your accountant needs it.
  3. 3
    Pick the tax year Choose the year you're reporting on from the date picker.
  4. 4
    Generate the report Click Generate. TradeBooks pulls all your categorized expenses and income for that period and calculates totals, net profit, and deductible amounts.
  5. 5
    Export to CSV Hit Export CSV and send the file to your accountant. They'll have everything they need — no more digging through shoeboxes.
    💡 Run this report quarterly, not just at year-end. You'll catch surprises early and stay ahead of estimated taxes.

Tips for Contractors

A few habits that make TradeBooks work even harder for you.

Pro Tip

Snap receipt photos immediately

Take the photo right when you pay — at the register, at the lumber yard, before you leave the parking lot. You'll never have to dig through a pile of crumpled receipts again, and every deduction is documented if the IRS ever asks.

Pro Tip

Always link expenses to a job

It only takes a second, but it's the difference between knowing whether a job made money versus just guessing. Per-job profitability is your most valuable business insight — don't skip this step.

Pro Tip

Invoice as soon as you hit a milestone

Don't wait until the job is done. Set up milestone-based invoices — deposit, rough-in, completion — and send each one the day you hit it. The faster you invoice, the faster you get paid.

Pro Tip

Run a quarterly tax check-in

Pull a Tax Summary report every three months. If you're making good money, you may owe estimated quarterly taxes — catching it early means no surprise bill in April. Your accountant will thank you.

Best Practice

Set a budget for every job

Even a rough number — your contract amount, or what you think materials and labor should cost. TradeBooks shows you actual vs. budget in real time. It's the earliest warning system you have for a job going sideways.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions that come up most.

How do I reset my password?

On the login screen, click "Forgot password?" below the sign-in button. Enter your email address and you'll receive a reset link within a few minutes. Check your spam folder if you don't see it.

If you're already logged in and want to change your password, go to Settings → Account and look for the password section.

Can I use TradeBooks on my phone?

Yes — TradeBooks is built mobile-first. Open it in your phone's browser and it adapts to a clean bottom-tab layout. You can also add it to your home screen for a native app feel: in Safari tap the Share button and choose Add to Home Screen, or in Chrome tap the menu and choose Install App.

Receipt photo capture works directly from your phone's camera — perfect for snapping a photo right at the job site.

How do I get my data ready for my accountant?

Go to Reports → Tax Summary, select the tax year, generate the report, then click Export CSV. The CSV file has all your income and expenses broken down by Schedule C category — exactly what your accountant needs.

Most contractors email this file to their accountant in January and they're done. Some accountants prefer a printed summary — you can also download the full report as a PDF.

What happens when I mark an invoice as paid?

The invoice status changes to Paid and the total amount is automatically recorded as income — linked to the right client and job. You don't have to enter it twice.

Your dashboard totals update immediately, and that income appears in your Reports. If you later need to un-mark an invoice as paid, just edit it and change the status back — the income record will update to match.

Can I add my own expense categories?

Yes. Go to Settings → Categories and you can add custom expense categories alongside the built-in Schedule C ones. Give each category a name and a color so it's easy to spot at a glance.

A quick note: the built-in categories (Materials, Subcontractors, Fuel, etc.) map directly to Schedule C lines — if you're aiming for clean tax reporting, it's worth using those where possible and reserving custom categories for things that are specific to your business.
